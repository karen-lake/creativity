OUTCOME

I started by wireframing each step in the online applications process, submitting revision after revision. What we started to discover was how the application wasn’t user focused at all. The interface was clunky and difficult to navigate, and you had no idea what step you were on during the process.

Then I started to journey map and work through the application’s required processes and tried to simplify them. Eventually it all became too convoluted and the redesign was cancelled and the application taken back to the drawing board.

While the redesign was a failure, I considered the outcome of going back to the drawing board a win for the end user. The developers had clearly not seen them as the most important factor during the application’s development.

ABOUT

I always wanted to work with computers – but doing what?? I thought drawing on them was kinda fun but that probably wasn’t going to cut it.

Twenty years later and I do draw on computers and make a living out of it. But being an all-rounder and annoyingly curious means I can do lots of other things too. Graduating originally as a Graphic Designer I have now moved into the Front End Developer realm - but still get a kick out of good design – and love being able to both design and build in the digital world.

For the last 15 years I have been working in tertiary education and semi-government sectors. Seven years of that was involved in producing digital assets, good old print collateral and multimedia animations. The other eight years of experience went to.... you guessed it designing, building and maintaining websites. As you can imagine I know have solid coding skills in HTML, CSS, SASS and LESS preprocessors and are now learning to program with Javascript.

My strong empathy for others means I love usability and accessibility standards, and with methodical problem-solving skills I never back down on solving a problem. Also, I’m an Adobe fangirl from way back, so I have advanced skills in the latest Adobe Creative Suite, including Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign and Dreamweaver.
